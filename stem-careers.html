cAREERS IN SCIENCE (S IN STEM):

1. PHYSICIST

A physicist produces scientific ideas and models to help them understand and explain the characteristics of the natural world, such as atom formations or the gravitational pull. They might research cosmological evolution as well as the underlying characteristics of atoms and molecules.

2. Astronomer

Astronomers study planets, stars, and other celestial bodies. They employ both space-based technology, such as the James Webb or Hubble Space Telescope, and ground-based technology, such as optical telescopes. Many astronomers explore far-off galaxies and objects like neutron stars and black holes.

3. Biochemist

Biochemists, often known as molecular or cellular biologists, can investigate the molecular processes behind cell growth, division, and nutrition. Others research plant and animal evolution to learn how genetic features are passed down through generations.

4. Materials scientist

To create new products or improve existing ones, materials scientists study the structures and chemical composition of diverse materials. They produce new materials for usage in a range of products or find ways to combine or reinforce currently available materials.

5. Geoscientist

To understand the past, present, and future of the Earth, geoscientists examine its physical characteristics, such as its composition, structure, and processes.

6. Chemist

Chemists and materials scientists investigate the interactions between different substances at the atomic and molecular levels. A lot of them put their knowledge to use by creating new, better products and evaluating the quality of manufactured goods.

7. Microbiologist

Microbiologists research many kinds of parasites, bacteria, viruses, algae, and fungi. They make an effort to understand how these species function, develop, and engage with their surroundings. Microbiologists may use this knowledge to develop life-saving vaccines and medicine, genetically engineered crops, and more.

8. Epidemiologist

Public health professionals known as epidemiologists look at the patterns and causes of illness and damage. Through research, community outreach, and public health initiatives, they aim to lower the risk and occurrence of adverse health consequences.

9. Anthropologist

The origin, evolution, and behavior of humans are topics of study for anthropologists and archaeologists. They look at the physical traits, archeological relics, and linguistic and cultural diversity around the world.

10. Computer and information research scientist

Researchers in computer and information science design new computing techniques and enhance the application of already existing technologies. They research complicated computing issues and are frequently pioneers in finding solutions for them in the domains of business, science, medicine, and other disciplines.

cAREERS IN TECHNOLOGY (t IN STEM):

12. Software developer

Computer programs are created, designed, and built by software developers. Some create brand-new desktop or mobile programs, while others create the core operating systems. In any case, software engineers analyze customer requirements, create applications, evaluate fresh software, and make adjustments.

13. Network/cloud architect

An IT expert in charge of directing a company's cloud computing strategy is known as a cloud architect. Plans for adopting the cloud, designing cloud applications, and managing and monitoring the cloud are all included.

14. Information security analyst

To secure computer networks, information security specialists install and customize different software, including firewalls and antivirus programs. The computer networks and systems of a company are protected by security measures that are planned and implemented by information security experts.

15. Computer systems analyst

A computer systems analyst is someone who enjoys using the full range of knowledge from science, engineering, and business. These technical positions create and put into action fixes for challenging network, system, or integration-related computer issues.

16. Software quality assurance analyst and tester

Software testers and analysts create and implement systems to examine the software for flaws. These workers record and keep track of any potential flaws or dangers in the program as part of their testing. They evaluate its usability and functionality as well to find any potential user-experience issues.

17. Database administrator

The information technician in charge of planning, carrying out, and overseeing all operations involved in successfully maintaining a database environment is known as a database administrator (DBA). A DBA ensures that a company's databases and related applications run effectively and functionally.

18. Computer programmer

Code and scripts are written by computer programmers, modified, and tested to ensure that software and applications work as intended. They typically, using their knowledge, convert the blueprints made by engineers and software developers into computer-readable code.

19. UX designer

UX designers make computer programs appealing and desirable for users to use. In order to increase usability and produce the greatest user experience, UX (user experience) designers evaluate and optimize apps, which are typically web-based. This is done by experimenting with a variety of techniques to address end-users' issues.

20. Web developer

The person who creates, modifies, and/or maintains a website is known as a web developer, also known as a web designer or webmaster. Web developers use their technical and creative abilities to build useful websites and web apps (such as an informational website, a product landing page, or the Twitter web app) for small businesses, huge enterprises, or as independent contractors.

cAREERS IN ENGINEERING (E IN STEM):

21. Computer hardware engineer

Engineers that work on computer hardware conduct research, design, development, and testing on computer systems and parts such circuit boards. Researchers, designers, developers, and testers of computer hardware, including processors, circuit boards, memory devices, networks, and routers.

22. Aerospace engineer

Aerospace engineers typically create designs and assess them to ensure that they adhere to engineering standards. Spacecraft, satellites, and missiles are primarily designed by aerospace engineers. They also develop prototypes and test them to make sure they work as intended.

23. Chemical engineer

Processes for manufacturing chemicals are created and designed by chemical engineers. To solve issues involving the manufacture or usage of chemicals, fuel, medications, food, and many other goods, chemical engineers use the concepts of chemistry, biology, physics, and math.

24. Electrical engineer

Electric motors, radar and navigation systems, communications systems, or equipment used in power generation are just a few examples of the electrical equipment that electrical engineers design, develop, test, and oversee the production of. The electrical systems of cars, airplanes, spacecraft, and more, are also created by electrical engineers.

25. Health and safety engineers

These engineers must combine their engineering expertise with their knowledge of health and safety to develop systems that safeguard individuals or property against disease, harm, or destruction.

26. Materials engineer

To design new materials, materials engineers deal with metals, ceramics, and polymers. To make a variety of items, from computer chips and aircraft wings to golf clubs and biomedical devices, materials engineers develop, process, and test the materials.

27. Biomedical engineer

In order to create new tools and machinery for enhancing human health, biomedical engineers concentrate on developments in technology and medicine. For instance, they might create computer simulations to evaluate new pharmacological regimens or software to run medical equipment.

28. Geological engineer

Geological engineers look for mineral deposits and assess potential locations. Once a location is chosen, they design an efficient and environmentally responsible method of extracting the metals or minerals. Mining engineers frequently specialize in a single commodity or mineral, such coal or gold.

29. Industrial engineer

Among their many responsibilities, industrial engineers create programs for employment evaluation. Industrial engineers look for solutions to get rid of waste in the manufacturing process. They create effective systems that combine labor, machinery, supplies, information, and energy to produce goods or render services.

30. Mechanical engineer

Mechanical engineers do research, design, development, construction, and testing on tools, engines, and other mechanical and thermal sensors and systems.

31. CIVIL ENGINEER

In both the public and commercial sectors, civil engineers conceptualize, design, build, supervise, run, and maintain infrastructure projects and systems, such as highways, buildings, airports, tunnels, dams, bridges, and water supply and sewage treatment systems.

32. Agricultural engineer

Agricultural engineers work to address challenges with power sources, machinery effectiveness, building and facility utilization, pollution and the environment, as well as the storage and processing of agricultural goods.

cAREERS IN MATHEMATICS (M IN STEM):

33. Mathematician

A mathematician is someone who applies their in-depth understanding of mathematics to their work, usually to address mathematical issues. Numbers, data, amount, structure, space, models, and change are all important to mathematicians.

34. Actuary

Actuaries examine the monetary costs associated with risk and uncertainty. They estimate the risk of prospective events using mathematics, statistics, and financial theory, and they assist clients and corporations in creating policies that reduce the price of that risk. The job of actuaries is crucial to the insurance sector.

35. Data scientist

A data scientist is a professional who uses analytical, statistical, and programming skills to gather significant volumes of data. It is their obligation to use data to create solutions that are specifically matched to the demands of the organization.

36. Statistician

A statistician collects numerical data, displays it, and assists businesses in making sense of the data, identifying trends, and making forecasts. Designing data acquisition trials and analyzing the outcomes are typical work duties.

37. Operations research analyst

Data is managed and evaluated by operations research analysts to enhance marketing, pricing, supply chains, and corporate operations. A greater range of applications for operations research are now possible and more cheap thanks to advancements in analytical software.
