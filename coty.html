CHINA'S SHANGHAI

THE MOST UP-TO-DATE CITY IN THE ENTIRE WORLD

HOME TO ABOUT THIRTY MILLION INDIVIDUALS

At the time of writing, approximately 30 million people live in the incredible city/province of Shanghai. That's more than Florida, Texas, New York, London, Bangkok, and most other cities. It's also nearly the same number of people who live in the countries of Canada or Australia. Shanghai's enormous and impressive metro system is essential to covering all of its districts and maintaining the daily operations of this glowing city. Shanghai is what all modern-day cities should look like. The only way to truly understand Shanghai's uniqueness and beauty is to visit it in-person.

The city that you'll never see sleep

The Chinese government clearly took to heart the phrase "city that never sleeps" when they set out to make Shanghai the (more modern and futuristic) New York of Asia. Because of China's aim for the city to develop into a major financial and cultural hub, it has experienced rapid growth during the past 20 years. Shanghai is home to many flourishing businesses that are giving both the expanding middle class in China and foreigners interested in entering the Chinese market new opportunities.

Shanghai features a unique mix of modernism and history. Shanghai was divided between British, U.S., and French territories and a Chinese walled city while it was a colonial territory in the middle of the nineteenth century. Due to the high concentration of Chinese residents in the foreign concessions, Shanghai developed a distinctive range of architectural styles and an interesting history.
