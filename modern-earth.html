EARTH NEEDS TO BE MODERNIZED, REMOVED OF OLD OUTDATED CITIES

The Atmos Modern earth concept (still crazy how this is still only a "concept") imagines a future on earth where all cities are up-to-date.

The sad reality is that the majority of cities which exist across all of the regions of Earth are painfully outdated. For example, just have a quick look at Paris on Google, in the image section. You'll find yourself looking at a heavily outdated cluster of horrible-looking buildings, for the most part. The same goes for Madrid in Spain, Istanbul in Turkey, the vast majority of cities in the US, Russia, Indonesia, and every other country. Even the capital of the United States, the country that most people consider to be the most powerful one, looks severely outdated in numerous aspects. Fortunately, countries such as China, the UAE, and just a few others, are building high-tech cities which use modern technologies and are visually up-to-date. However, 3 or 4 countries is simply not enough. The whole world needs to be participating in this effort to modernize all cities, so we don't end up living in the 1900s forever. I don't know about you, but knowing what we're capable of in the 21st century, I wouldn't want to be living in ancient Rome forever. I know that we're so much more capable than we currently seem, and I don't want to be living in the same old and outdated time period forever, because I know we can do so much more. We urge you to help advance humanity from a technological perspective by pursuing a career in STEM, so you don't live in an "ancient Rome" forever.

Now, just for a minute, let's forget about this ancient Rome stuff. Try to imagine a world where every city you can think of on the globe (and across the solar system) is utilizing advanced modern-day technologies, is actually visually appealing, is efficient, and allows for fast transportation. That's a future that we should all strive to live in, because why would you want to live in a future full of highly outdated and inefficient cities knowing we are capable of transforming them into highly efficient, visually appealing, up-to-date cities? We think it's absolutely absurd that Atmos has to call this a "concept", because we've had so much time to start creating modern cities across the globe, and across the solar system. It's been about 50 years since we first landed a human being on another world - the Moon, yet, we haven't even sent humans beyond Earth since. If you asked someone in 1969, when Apollo 11 launched, about how the year 2023 might look, there's a very good chance that they would have said "many big cities on the moon, flying cars (or something of the sort), a futuristic sci-fi looking Earth, robots controlling all stores, and more", however, we haven't come close to any of those. No people on the Moon, let alone cities, nothing like flying cars, no futuristic-looking Earth, no robots controlling stores, etc. The average person likely would have predicted those things, yet we don't even have modern cities everywhere across Earth now. That needs to change.

MODERN AND FAST PUBLIC TRANSPORTATION

Perhaps the extremely modern subways of China aren't as efficient as they could be (from an engineering perspective), however, it's very up-to-date, uses modern technologies, and is visually appealing. This should be the standard for all subway systems across the world, no matter the country. But instead, we have old, run-down, inefficient, dangerous subways, such as the ones in NYC. Just think of that: the world's most powerful country, with the biggest and most powerful economy, runs on old, dangerous, and inefficient subways built decades ago. This should change, and the rest of the world's cities should all be as technologically advanced as China's.

A LIMITLESS AMT. OF JOB OPPORTUNITIES

Cities like New York already have plenty of job opportunities, however, large, modern, and developed cities with fast transportation, plenty of buildings, and more, would have even more job opportunities. Jobs don't only provide income for people to live their daily lives, but they're also what drives humanity and pushes it forward. Jobs are responsible for the world's biggest scientific breakthroughs, and pretty much everything else you've known. Large, modern, and developed cities, would be major centers of innovation and job opportunity. Modern cities are extremely important!

HUMANS ARE inherently SOCIAL CREATURES

As widely known, human beings are social animals. We need each other to live. Modern cities are home to millions of people, packed densely together. Whenever you walk around in such a city, there are countless people walking everywhere. This gives you more chances to interact with people across the city, meet new people, make friends, and more. So, not only are cities centers of innovation & development; they are also places that contain such a large amount of people, that you could easily find dozens of new friends there. NYC may have its many problems, but it's a great example for this.

MODERN CITIES ARE HOME TO EVERYTHING YOU NEED

From restaurants to zoos to global financial centers, modern cities have sections that contain every type of place you can think of. For example, New York City has one area which contains the One World Trade Center skyscraper which many businesses are inside of, the Three World Financial Center which is home to the headquarters of American Express. Then, New York City has another area called Chinatown, which is home to countless bakeries, bubble tea shops, restaurants, pharmacies, and more. Modern cities have just about everything you'll ever need, including nature (see Central Park).
