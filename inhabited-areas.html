WE MUST EXPAND TO OTHER WORLDS.

FAILURE TO DO SO WILL ULTIMATELY RESULT IN THE SAD EXTINCTION OF THE HUMAN RACE, FOREVER.

The celestial bodies listed below are POSSIBLE candidates for human colonization

MARS, HUMANITY'S SOLAR SYSTEM, LANIAKEA

No brainer, really. Within the next few decades, we need to colonize Mars as quickly and intelligently as we can. The long-term survival of humanity is only now taking its first tentative step.

Some possible locations for cities and/or bases include:
- (Near) Korolev Crater

EUROPA, JUPITER SYSTEM, HUMANITY'S SOLAR SYSTEM, LANIAKEA

One of 80 known moons of Jupiter, Europa, could be an ideal place for humanity to build self-sustaining cities. Europa has an ocean underneath it's surface, which consists of liquid water. This water can be used for many things, including fuel, oxidizer, drinking water, & more.

Titan, saturn system, humanity's solar system, laniakea

The moon named Titan, orbiting gas-giant planet Saturn, could be a good place for human colonization. This Moon, smaller than Earth, has all of the necessary components to sustain biological life, (likely) including liquid water beneath its surface, a nitrogen-rich thick atmosphere, and much more.

CALLISTO, JUPITER SYSTEM, HUMANITY'S SOLAR SYSTEM, LANIAKEA

The second-largest moon of Jupiter, Callisto, may be a good candidate for human colonization. This moon is geologically stable and has low radiation levels. Callisto may also have a subsurface water ocean approximately 250 kilometers below its surface.

TRITON, NEPTUNE SYSTEM, HUMANITY'S SOLAR SYSTEM, LANIAKEA

The largest moon of the planet Neptune in Humanity's Solar System could be a good candidate for human colonization, because, like other moons in this list, there is evidence of water beneath its surface. This moon also has nitrogen and methane, both being incredibly useful.

ENCELADUS, SATURN SYSTEM, HUMANITY'S SOLAR SYSTEM, LANIAKEA

The sixth-largest moon of Saturn is named Enceladus. It has a diameter of 500 kilometers, which is about a tenth of Titan, Saturn's biggest moon. Enceladus is one of the Solar System's most reflective bodies because it is largely covered in recent, pure ice. This ice could be mined by humans.

CERES, HUMANITY'S SOLAR SYSTEM, LANIAKEA

Ceres is a suitable location for a future colony because it is the largest body in the asteroid belt. Resources mined from the Belt could be transported to Earth, Mars, or other spacecraft by using Ceres as the principal base and transportation hub for asteroid mining. Ceres could be used for spacecraft refueling as well.

GANYMEDE, JUPITER SYSTEM, HUMANITY'S SOLAR SYSTEM, LANIAKEA

The Galileo spacecraft discovered that Ganymede is the only moon known to have its own magnetic field. Around its north and south poles, the magnetic field produces auroras, which are ribbons of blazing, hot, electrified gas. Furthermore, the magnetic field offers defense against Jupiter's radiation belts. In addition, a large saltwater ocean subsurface is thought to exist on Ganymede.

Out of all the celestial bodies listed above which could potentially be colonized by humans in the near future, even the farthest one, Triton, could be reached in around 3 and a half days, if we could travel at just 5% the speed of light (14,989,622 m/s).

If you want to be part of a team that potentially could figure out how to achieve such a velocity using currently-unknown advanced propulsion technologies, we strongly encourage you to pursue a career in the field of STEM. Our future lies in the cosmos.

More DISCOVERIES, more progress

If humanity is spread out across the galaxy's many worlds and is actively procreating, our species will grow exponentially. Every new human being born has the potential to contribute to a significant advancement in STEM. And because there will be so many new humans on countless planets every minute, there is a much greater chance that a human will make a new, important discovery or invention. Additionally, even if none of those people—which is impossible—make a substantial advancement, humanity would still advance more quickly every day.

Effectively avoiding human extinction

A single planet species, like humanity in 2023, is extremely vulnerable. If the human race expands out far into the galaxy and occupies countless celestial bodies, our chances of extinction are essentially zero. Let's say that humanity is living across 900 planets in the galaxy. Even if 899 of those planets were to somehow be destroyed, which is virtually impossible, humanity would still exist on 1 planet, and can begin expanding again. The more planets we expand to, the less of a chance there is of humanity going extinct.

UTILIZING THE RESOURCES OF OTHER PLANETS

Asteroids contain valuable resources, such as water and precious metals, that could be used for space exploration and other purposes. Asteroid mining could help reduce our dependence on resources from Earth, provide an almost limitless supply of resources, and advance our scientific knowledge and technology. It could also support space-based industries and create new job opportunities. Overall, the ability to access the valuable resources contained within asteroids could be an important step in the expansion of human civilization into space.

BUILDING MAJOR CITIES ACROSS THE SOL. SYSTEM

We discussed on our Modern Earth page that major cities which are dense (thousands of buildings, all close together, like in NYC on Earth) are beneficial in numerous ways. They provide modern & fast public transportation, a limitless amount of job opportunities, many opportunities for social interaction, and provide all of the basic necessities in life. Well, imagine if such major cities were located all across the solar system on various moons & planets, and not just Earth. It'd be amazing. Lots of money being generated, lots of innovation, lots of business, and countless other things!
